Pasang Lhamu Sherpa

1961-1993

First Nepalese female ascent

It has been 29 years since Pasang Lhamu Sherpa climbed the world's highest peak, Mount Everest. She climbed Mt. Everest on 22 April, 1993, representing all Nepali women. Earlier, in 1975, Junko Tabei, a Japanese woman, became the first woman to climb Mt Everest. However, no Nepalese woman had achieved this feat back then. In this connection, Pasang Lhamu did not dare to climb Mt. Everest. Pasang Lhamu was born in the district of Solukhumbu on 16 December, 1961. As a child, she started trekking in the mountains following her father, Late Phurvakitar Sherpa. Later, when she became an owner of a trekking company, her love for mountains grew. In this process, she had even climbed some mountains at home and abroad like Mount Cho Oyu, Yala Peak, Pisang and Mount Blanc.

Biographies

  • Born on December 10, 1961, in the village of Lung, Solu-Khumbu region, Nepal.
  • Came from a family with a strong mountaineering background.
  • Dreamed of becoming a mountaineer from a young age.
  • Faced societal norms and gender biases that discouraged women from mountaineering.
  • Limited access to education and resources in her village.
  • Started her career as a mountaineer, participating in several climbing expeditions.
  • Gained recognition for her skills and determination.
  • n 1992, embarked on an Everest expedition but tragically lost her life in a severe storm.
  • Her body was found at 8,300 meters, marking the highest point reached by a woman at the time.
  • The Pasang Lhamu Mountaineering Foundation was established in her honor.
